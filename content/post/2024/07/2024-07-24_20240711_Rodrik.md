<p>Dani Rodrik.</p>
<ul>
<li>
<blockquote>The left has yet to forge an identity that is fit for current realities. How should they reposition themselves? Should they focus on redistribution, as the NFP in France appears to have done? Should they uphold fiscal responsibility, like the UK’s Labour Party? Should they embrace industrial policies à la Biden, and to what purpose? How should they handle issues such as immigration, the environment, or transgender rights, on which the cultural elite hold very different views from the general public?<br />左派は、現在の現実にふさわしいアイデンティティをまだ確立していない。左派はどのようなポジションを再構築すべきなのだろうか。フランスのNFPのように再分配を重視するのか。イギリスの労働党のように財政責任を堅持すべきか。バイデンのように産業政策を取り入れるべきなのか。移民、環境、トランスジェンダーの権利など、文化的エリートが一般大衆とはまったく異なる見解を持つ争点をどう扱うべきなのか。</blockquote>
</li>
<li>
<blockquote>
<p>The difficulty is that old strategies will not work. Unionized workers in manufacturing formed the core of support for leftist parties in the decades following World War II. They were also the foundation of the middle class.</p>
<p>Today manufacturing employs an ever-smaller share of workers in the US and Europe. The bulk of the workforce is in services. When Biden took office in January 2021, the manufacturing share of US employment had already shrunk to 8.5%. Today it is below 8.2%, despite all his administration’s efforts to revitalize manufacturing. Some European countries, such as Germany, have higher shares of manufacturing employment, but none has managed to avoid a decline over time.</p>
<p>Parties of the left have not yet fully faced this fact. None of their talk about reshoring, competitiveness, digitalization, and the green transition sounds realistic when it comes to jobs. Nor does protectionism against China. Strategies that focus on manufacturing have considerably less political appeal when most workers are not in manufacturing and have no realistic prospect of being employed there.</p>
難しいのは、古い戦略が通用しないということだ。第二次世界大戦後の数十年間、製造業の組合員労働者が左翼政党支持の中核を形成していた。彼らはまた、中産階級の基盤でもあった。<br />今日、アメリカやヨーロッパでは、製造業が労働者に占める割合はますます小さくなっている。労働力の大半はサービス業である。2021年1月にバイデンが大統領に就任したとき、アメリカの雇用に占める製造業の割合はすでに8.5％まで縮小していた。バイデン政権が製造業を活性化させようと努力しているにもかかわらず、現在では8.2％を下回っている。ドイツなど一部の欧州諸国は製造業雇用の割合が高いが、長期にわたって減少を避けられた国はない。<br />左派政党はこの事実をまだ十分に直視していない。リショアリング、競争力強化、デジタル化、グリーン・トランジション（環境保護への移行）など、雇用に関する彼らの話はどれも現実的ではない。対中保護主義もそうだ。ほとんどの労働者が製造業に従事しておらず、そこで雇用される現実的な見込みがない以上、製造業に焦点を当てた戦略は政治的アピール力をかなり低下させる。</blockquote>
</li>
<li>
<blockquote>Redistributive policies also have problems. There is a strong argument for making tax systems more progressive and increasing tax rates on top income earners. Higher social transfers and better social insurance would help, especially in the US, where social safety nets remain weak. But income transfers do not compensate workers for the loss of dignity and social recognition that accompany the disappearance of good jobs. Nor do they repair the breakdown in social and community life that ensues when factories close or move elsewhere.</blockquote>
</li>
<li>
<blockquote>What the left needs, then, is a credible program of creating good, productive jobs throughout the economy – especially in lagging regions and for workers with less than a university education. The representative target of such a program is not an auto or steel worker, but a care or retail worker.Moreover, labor-friendly innovation must be at the center of the program. Boosting wages and jobs at the same time requires organizational and technological innovations that enhance the productivity of less-educated workers. Unlike automation and other forms of labor-saving technologies, labor-friendly innovations help ordinary workers perform a greater range of more complicated tasks. Digital tools that confer expertise are an example.<br />経済全体にわたって、特に遅れた地域や大学教育以下の労働者のために、良質で生産的な雇用を創出する信頼できるプログラム、労働者に配慮した組織的・技術的イノベーション（省力化技術ではなく）</blockquote>
</li>
<li>
<blockquote>
<p>A new left must confront head-on both the new structure of the economy and the productivity imperative. Only then will it turn into the genuine political movement of the future and a credible alternative to the far right.</p>
<p>新たな左派は、経済の新しい構造と生産性の要請の両方に正面から立ち向かわなければならない。そうすることで初めて、未来の真の政治運動となり、極右に代わる信頼できる選択肢となるのである。</p>
</blockquote>
</li>
</ul>
