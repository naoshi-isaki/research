<p>British lessons. There is a lot to learn and ponder about the historic electoral victory by the Labour Party in Britain:</p>

<p>First, British political norms should be a lesson to American politicians. Rishi Sunak graciously accepted defeat and congratulated Keir 
Starmer: https://youtube.com/watch?v=hPdpemrGmg8&amp;ab_channel=GuardianNews<br />It is difficult to imagine a situation in which Donald Trump will do so if he loses the election in November. But civil society, media and pro-democracy politicians around the world have to put more pressure on Donald Trump and his allies to roll back this type of anti-democratic behavior.<br />Second, how Keir Starmer brought the Labour Party back from its worst defeat in decades to its most decisive victory is 
notable: he emphasized moderation and improving the economy and public services. This is a lesson to mainstream politicians in Europe and the United States. It is possible to win elections without extremism and by promising to make democracy work better. I believe the left has much more to learn from Starmer than Jeremy Corbyn’s incoherent extremism.<br />Third, now Starmer and the Labour Party have to deliver, and if they do, this could be a role model for other center-left politicians and parties. If they fail, this would be a big setback. The UK has a huge productivity problem. The economy has not had much of productivity growth and only anemic economic growth since the financial crisis. This is not an easy problem to tackle, but more public and private sector investment will help, and so will a coherent industrial strategy prioritizing innovation and socially beneficial technology development.<br />Fourth, it must be admitted that the electoral system greatly helped the Labour Party. As the graphic above shows, Labour received 34% of the vote and is expected to receive 64% of the seats in Parliament.<br />https://ichef.bbci.co.uk/news/1024/cpsprodpb/9e00/live/c9387130-3ad1-11ef-a044-9d4367d5b599.png.webp</p>
<p>Fifth, and relatedly, this election is as much about Nigel Farage and his new Reform UK party. Farage’s party received 14% of the vote, compared to the 24% vote share of the Conservative Party. If the Tories had captured all of Reform UK’s vote, they would have won the election with 38%, compared to Labour’s 34%. As in the rest of Europe, there will be growing pressure for the center-right to tilt further to the right in the UK. Labour and the center have to be prepared for this challenge, and they must develop their own reasonable narrative and policies on immigration, which is one of the main issues motivating people to vote for Reform UK and other hard-right parties around Europe.</p>

<p>Daron: </p>
<p>Correct, there is a lot to learn from the Labour victory. </p>
<p>Unfortunately, this is not it! </p>
<p>A short thread 
why: ...</p>
<p>1) In fact,  the % Labour vote under the moderate Starmer (33.7%) in 2024 was a lot LESS than in 2017 under radical leftie Corbyn (40%) -- and very similar to a series of GEs won by the Con since 2010.</p>
<p><img src="https://pbs.twimg.com/media/GSLKg4SWsAAS5F_?format=png&amp;name=medium" alt="画僁E /></p>
<p>2) So what explains the outcome? Three factors.</p>
<p>First, the Con vote fell to its worst ever in its long history because of government incompetence, poor delivery of public goods &amp; services, unstable leadership, disillusionment with Brexit, many scandals, a poor campaign, etc.</p>
<p><img src="https://pbs.twimg.com/media/GSLMm0BXMAAfSJX?format=jpg&amp;name=medium" alt="画僁E /></p>
<p>3) Competition matters. In 2019, Reform didn't nominate candidates in Con-held seats. But in 2024, Reform ran 609 candidates nationwide. The net result split the rightwing vote, letting Lab win seats, esp. in the 'red wall' north.</p>
<p><img src="https://pbs.twimg.com/media/GSLOxXgX0AAbzzk?format=png&amp;name=small" alt="画僁E /></p>
<p>4) Lastly, it's vital to understand the FPTP electoral system, tactical voting, and efficient strategic campaigning, which gave the largest 'winner's bonus' to Lab in the postwar era.</p>
<p><img src="https://pbs.twimg.com/media/GSLPPYSWUAEXwcS?format=png&amp;name=900x900" alt="画僁E /></p>
<p>5) In conclusion, you assume that Labour's landslide of seats must be due to the appeal of Starmer's moderate and cautious policies. Nope. This probably didn't hurt Lab, but neither did it expand their popular vote. </p>
